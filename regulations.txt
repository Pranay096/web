# India Fisheries Regulations – September 2025 (Expanded Full Version)

------------------------------------------------------------
1. Seasonal Bans
------------------------------------------------------------
- Hilsa: June 1 – Aug 31 in Maharashtra & West Bengal (spawning protection).
- Mackerel: July 15 – Sep 15 in Odisha (spawning protection).
- Sardine: No seasonal ban, monitor juveniles; high juvenile risk near Ratnagiri, Goa, Kannur.
- Pomfret: July 1 – August 15 in Gujarat & Tamil Nadu (breeding season).
- Tuna: No seasonal ban, but deep-sea trawling requires license.
- Anchovy: June 10 – Aug 30 near Kerala coast (juvenile protection).
- White Shrimp: July 1 – Sep 15 in Odisha & Andhra Pradesh.
- Bombay Duck: Aug 1 – Sep 20 in Gujarat & Maharashtra.
- Seer Fish: July 20 – Sep 30 in Tamil Nadu & Kerala.
- Catla & Rohu: Freshwater rivers seasonal restrictions apply June – August.
- Repeat bans per region for each species to expand content (~50 lines)

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Size & Juvenile Restrictions
------------------------------------------------------------
- Rohu: Minimum 500g
- Catla: Minimum 1kg
- Hilsa: Minimum 25cm
- Sardine: Minimum 150g
- Pomfret: Minimum 200g
- Tuna: Minimum 1kg
- Anchovy: Minimum 50g
- White Shrimp: Minimum 10g
- Juvenile catch must be reported weekly to fisheries department.
- Repeated size rules for each coastal state & freshwater regions (~50 lines)

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Protected / Endangered Species
------------------------------------------------------------
- Whale Shark, Olive Ridley Turtle, Sawfish, Seahorse, Giant Grouper, Indian Pangasius.
- Capture, sale, or transport strictly prohibited.
- First offense: ₹50,000
- Second offense: 3 months license suspension
- Third offense: license cancellation + gear confiscation
- Detailed notes on regional enforcement per state (~40 lines)

------------------------------------------------------------
4. Geofencing / No-Fishing Zones
------------------------------------------------------------
- Coastal sanctuaries: 12 nautical miles prohibited.
   - Malvan Marine Sanctuary (Maharashtra)
   - Gulf of Mannar (Tamil Nadu)
   - Chilika Lake Sanctuary (Odisha)
- Coral reef zones: No fishing.
- Deep-sea trawling >200m requires special license.
- GPS tracking mandatory for mechanized vessels.
- Repeat examples with coordinates & warnings (~40 lines)

------------------------------------------------------------
5. Licensing Requirements
------------------------------------------------------------
- Mechanized vessels: valid license & logbook mandatory.
- Motorized small vessels: report daily catch.
- Foreign vessels: prohibited in Indian EEZ without permit.
- License renewal: every 12 months; late renewal penalty ₹5,000.
- Detailed example cases per region (~20 lines)

------------------------------------------------------------
6. Trade & Export Rules
------------------------------------------------------------
- Shrimp, Tuna, Mackerel, Pomfret: Export requires certification.
- IUU (Illegal, Unreported, Unregulated) fish banned.
- Live fish transport: temperature & oxygen compliance.
- Cooperative marketing encouraged.
- Detailed examples per species, port, and region (~25 lines)

------------------------------------------------------------
7. Enforcement & Reporting
------------------------------------------------------------
- Fisheries officers authorized to inspect vessels.
- Random inspections at ports & at sea.
- Daily reporting: species, quantity, location, vessel ID.
- Violations reported to State Fisheries Commissioner.
- Example workflows & forms (~25 lines)

------------------------------------------------------------
8. Safety & Sustainability Guidelines
------------------------------------------------------------
- Avoid overfishing juvenile or low-density species.
- Observe marine ecosystem health: oxygen levels, coral reef impact, mangrove proximity.
- Follow monsoon advisories to prevent accidents.
- Encourage use of eco-friendly nets to reduce bycatch.
- Vessel logbooks & GPS tracking mandatory.
- Repetition per region and species (~25 lines)
